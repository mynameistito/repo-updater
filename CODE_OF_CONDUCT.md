# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We as maintainers and contributors are committed to making participation in this project a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to acting in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected behavior

- **Be respectful.** Treat everyone with courtesy and professionalism, even in disagreement.
- **Be constructive.** Offer actionable feedback. Critique ideas and code, never people.
- **Be collaborative.** Share knowledge openly. Help others understand your reasoning.
- **Assume good intent.** Start from the assumption that others are here to contribute meaningfully.
- **Accept feedback gracefully.** Being asked to revise your work is normal — it is not a personal attack.
- **Stay on topic.** Keep issues, PRs, and discussions focused on the work at hand.
- **Acknowledge contributions.** Credit others for their ideas and work.

### Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Insulting, demeaning, or derogatory comments — including personal attacks or political hostility.
- Dismissing or belittling contributors for their experience level or background.
- Unwelcome sexual attention, advances, or language.
- Deliberate misgendering or use of rejected names.
- Publishing others' private information (addresses, emails, etc.) without explicit consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions — derailing threads, spamming, or bad-faith arguments.
- Retaliating against someone who reports a concern in good faith.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Code review comments
- Any other venue where you are acting as a representative or participant of this project

It also applies outside project spaces when an individual's behavior could reasonably affect the safety or well-being of community members.

---

## Reporting

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by using GitHub's private contact options to reach the maintainer directly. All reports will be:

- Reviewed promptly and taken seriously
- Handled with confidentiality
- Investigated fairly, giving all parties a chance to provide context

If you are unsure whether something qualifies, err on the side of reporting. You will not be penalized for reporting in good faith.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. When a violation is confirmed, maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

1. **Warning** — A private written notice explaining the violation and expected correction.
2. **Temporary restriction** — Temporary removal of the ability to interact in project spaces.
3. **Permanent ban** — Permanent removal from all project spaces for severe, repeated, or harmful violations.

Maintainers who do not uphold or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face consequences as determined by the project leadership.
